ABOUT

Being brought up with two contrasting cultures, I constantly struggle with the sense of alienation. Being raised in a Japanese household and under an American educational system, I have become a hybrid of the two, not a “halfie” but into one that is neither; an Alien of both cultures.

My recent works explore my thoughts and perspective of the two contrasting cultures and pose a question rather than an answer. These works initially started as meditation paintings where I express my suppressed thoughts and feelings. The works have now developed into a body of work where I revisit my core and reconstruct my identity through the process of painting. My interests have now shifted towards the fall of Japan; a meltdown the nation is now facing. The nuclear meltdown which occurred in 2011, the meltdown happening within the people and 　the meltdown of the international mind all happening simultaneously is a substantial complication our generation must overcome.

In retrospect, I have come to the realization that while growing up, I have almost exclusively been exposed to ‘flat’, 2-dimensional works only. Japanese animation, manga, traditional woodblock prints and even adverts I saw on a daily basis were illustrated. This has heavily influenced my recent interest and direction in my work as I had overcome this obsession of attempting in ‘adapting’ to the Western art conventions of 3-dimensional works which heavily contrast to the Eastern conventions. When creating these works, I am primarily focused on the ‘flatness’ not only in literal terms but also conceptually. Despite the various complications, the Japanese when talking about their nation have a tendency of ‘flattening’ their issues, ‘camouflaging’ them as if everything is good and that Japan is at its best. Perhaps, I am also guilty of this as I create the works through fragmented shapes in hopes that the fragments will once again gather and form a reconstructed figure.
